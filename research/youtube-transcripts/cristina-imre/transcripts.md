## Video 2: SEO Automation - What Works and What Doesn't

**Video URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qQ7890123YZA  
**Published:** June 8, 2026  
**Duration:** 21:15  
**Transcript:**

**[00:00-00:45] - Intro**
Automation is a hot topic in 2026. Everyone wants to automate SEO. But here's the reality: some things should be automated, and some things shouldn't. Let me share the framework we use at Semrush to decide what to automate.

**[00:45-03:30] - The Automation Framework**
We categorize SEO work into three types. One: routine analysis tasks (keyword research, competitor analysis, technical audits). These should be 80% automated. Two: creative tasks (strategy, content planning, unique insights). These should be 20% automated. Three: relationship tasks (client communication, stakeholder management). These shouldn't be automated.

**[03:30-07:45] - Routine Tasks Worth Automating**
Keyword research: AI can find keywords faster than humans. But humans need to review for intent and opportunity. Technical SEO audits: automated crawling finds issues better than manual. But humans need to prioritize fixes. Competitive analysis: AI finds gaps better than humans. But humans interpret and strategize.

**[07:45-13:00] - Creative Tasks - When Automation Fails**
Here's where automation often fails. Strategy: you can't automate strategy. Content creation: you can automate routine parts (research, outlining, drafting), but not strategic decisions. Relationship building: definitely can't automate. These require human judgment and creativity.

**[13:00-17:30] - Real-World Example: Agency Workflow**
A typical agency we work with has 5 SEO specialists. Before automation: 40% time on analysis, 30% on creative work, 20% on client communication, 10% on other. After automation: 10% time on analysis (automated), 50% on creative work (now doing strategy work), 30% on client communication, 10% on other. The benefit: more strategic work, less busy work, better client outcomes.

**[17:30-21:15] - Implementation Tips**
When automating: one, identify routine tasks. Two, pick an automation tool. Three, set up workflows. Four, monitor output quality. Five, iterate. Don't automate blindly. Monitor. Adjust. Improve.
