## Video 1: Keyword Research in 2026 - The Complete Framework

**Video URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jJ0123456PQR  
**Published:** June 16, 2026  
**Duration:** 23:18  
**Transcript:**

**[00:00-00:45] - Intro**
Hey Tim here with Ahrefs. This is probably one of the most important videos I've made because keyword research has fundamentally changed in 2026. If you're still doing keyword research the way you did it in 2024, you're missing opportunities and wasting effort. So I'm going to walk you through the modern keyword research framework that actually works right now.

**[00:45-03:30] - Why Traditional Keyword Research is Incomplete**
Traditional keyword research says: find high-volume keywords with low competition, optimize for them, and you'll rank. But that's missing huge parts of the picture. First, Search Generative Experiences (SGE) are showing answers directly, which reduces CTR for info queries. Second, user intent has become more complex. Third, topical relationships matter more than individual keywords. So you need a more comprehensive framework.

**[03:30-07:15] - The Modern Keyword Research Process**
Here's the new framework. Step one: understand customer journey. What problems does your product solve? How do customers typically find you? Step two: research customer language. What words do they actually use? Not what you think they use. Interviews, surveys, search console data. Step three: analyze competitors. What keywords are they ranking for? Where are they winning and losing? Step four: identify high-intent opportunities. These are keywords that indicate purchase intent or problem-solving intent.

**[07:15-12:30] - Understanding Search Intent in 2026**
Intent is everything in 2026. There are four types of intent: informational (learning), navigational (finding), transactional (buying), and commercial (comparing). Each needs different content. With SGE, informational content is getting hit hardest because SGE answers those queries directly. So you need to focus on high-intent queries: commercial and transactional. These show 2-3x better CTR with SGE than informational queries.

**[12:30-16:45] - Practical Tools and Techniques**
Now, practically, how do you research intent and find high-intent keywords? Use Ahrefs Site Explorer. Enter competitor domains, find their top-performing pages, look at the keywords driving them. Focus on keywords with commercial intent. Use Keyword Gap analysis to find keywords your competitors rank for but you don't. Use Question Analysis to find specific questions customers are asking—these are high-intent.

**[16:45-20:00] - Content Strategy from Keywords**
Once you have keywords, strategy is about clustering and content planning. Group related keywords into content clusters. One pillar page covering the broad topic, multiple cluster pages covering specific angles. This is way more effective than one page per keyword. And focus on topical authority. Ranking for one keyword in a cluster is less valuable than ranking for all keywords in a cluster.

**[20:00-23:18] - Putting It Into Practice**
Actionable steps: one, audit your current strategy. Are you focused on high-intent keywords? Two, do competitor analysis. What are they not covering? Three, plan topical clusters. Four, create content that covers topics comprehensively, not just individual keywords. Five, measure impact on business metrics, not just traffic. That's the modern approach. Check the full guide in the description. Thanks for watching.
