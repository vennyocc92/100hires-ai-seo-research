**Transcript:**

**[00:00-00:50] - Intro**
Hey everyone, Rand here with SparkToro. One of the biggest mistakes I see companies make is they skip audience research. They think they know their audience, and they jump straight to content creation. But here's the reality: companies that do comprehensive audience research see better results across every metric. Content engagement up 3x, conversion rates up 2x, product market fit clarity increases dramatically. So today I'm going to walk you through exactly how to do audience research in 2026, including how to use new AI tools to speed up the process.

**[00:50-04:15] - Why Audience Research Matters More Than Ever**
In 2026, audience research is more valuable than ever. Here's why. First, attention is more scarce. There's more content competing for attention than ever before. Second, AI is making content production faster, but it can't target effectively without knowing the audience. Third, companies are shifting from volume strategies to quality strategies, and that requires knowing exactly who you're trying to reach. Fourth, personalization is becoming standard, not optional. And personalization requires audience data.

**[04:15-08:30] - The Five Components of Audience Research**
Good audience research has five components. One: demographics. Age, location, income, education, industry. Two: psychographics. Values, interests, motivations, pain points. Three: behavioral data. What sites do they visit? What content do they consume? What tools do they use? Four: competitive landscape. Who else are they considering? What are competitors getting right or wrong? Five: priority markers. What's their biggest pain point? What keeps them up at night? What are they actively trying to solve right now?

**[08:30-12:45] - Traditional Research Methods (Still Valuable)**
Traditional methods are still incredibly valuable. Surveys, interviews, user testing, community engagement. For interviews, talk to 10-15 people in your target audience and ask open-ended questions. Most companies do this badly—they ask leading questions. Instead, ask: "What's your biggest challenge with [domain]?" "How are you solving it today?" "What would an ideal solution look like?" Document their exact words. This is gold.

**[12:45-16:00] - AI-Powered Audience Research (New Methods)**
Now, where AI helps is in scaling and pattern recognition. You can use AI to analyze audience discussions, identify trends, and categorize pain points. We built SparkToro specifically for this. The tool aggregates where your audience gets information, what they're saying about your space, and what they're interested in. It also analyzes competitor audiences to find gaps. AI makes this possible at scale in days instead of weeks.

**[16:00-20:15] - Creating Your Audience Personas**
Once you have research data, create detailed personas. Not just "35-year-old marketing manager." That's useless. Real personas include: name, title, company size, specific pain points with examples, information sources, objections to your product, and success metrics they care about. Give your persona a name. Make them real. This helps everyone in the organization align on who you're serving.

**[20:15-24:00] - Turning Research Into Strategy**
Now here's where most companies fail. They do research and then don't use it. To actually leverage your audience research, create a "research snapshot" document. What did we learn? What does this mean for our content? What should we stop doing? What should we start doing? Share this across your organization. Content team, product team, sales team, everyone should read this document.

**[24:00-26:15] - Outro and Resources**
That's how you do audience research in 2026. Check the description for links to research templates and SparkToro's free tools. If you want the advanced version, we have a full course. Thanks for watching. Don't forget to subscribe.

Hey everyone, Rand again. I want to talk about a shift I'm seeing in the industry. We're moving away from "content marketing" as we know it toward "audience-driven marketing." And this shift has huge implications for how companies should be thinking about strategy in 2026.

**[00:45-03:30] - The Old Model vs. The New Model**
The old model: pick a channel (blog, YouTube, email), create content, optimize for that channel, measure traffic. The new model: deeply understand your audience, identify their journey, create content that serves each stage of that journey across all channels, measure impact on business goals.

**[03:30-07:00] - Why the Shift is Happening**
Three reasons. One: channels are becoming less important. Content performs differently for different audiences. Some audiences prefer blogs, others prefer podcasts, others prefer TikTok. You have to know your audience to know which channels to use. Two: AI is making content creation easier, which means differentiation comes from audience insight, not content volume. Three: attention is limited, so you have to be precisely targeting your audience or you'll waste money.

**[07:00-11:30] - Audience Stages and Content Alignment**
With audience-first thinking, you map audience stages. Awareness stage: they're just discovering problems. Content strategy: educational, problem-definition focused. Consideration stage: they're evaluating solutions. Content strategy: comparison, best practices, case studies. Decision stage: they're choosing a vendor. Content strategy: demos, trials, ROI calculators. Each stage has different content needs.

**[11:30-16:00] - Technology Enablement**
The tools that enable this are getting better. Platforms like SparkToro help you understand audiences. Platforms like HubSpot and Marketo let you track audience segments across channels. AI writing tools let you create content variants quickly. The stack is coming together to make audience-driven marketing possible at scale.

**[16:00-20:15] - Putting It Into Practice**
How do you start? One: do audience research. Two: map audience stages. Three: audit existing content against stages. Four: identify gaps. Five: create content for gaps. Six: organize content by audience segment. Seven: test and measure. Eight: iterate. This is a journey, not a project.
