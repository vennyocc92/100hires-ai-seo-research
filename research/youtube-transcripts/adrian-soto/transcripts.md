## Video 1: ChatGPT for SEO - Complete Practical Guide

**Video URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vV2345678IJK  
**Published:** June 15, 2026  
**Duration:** 25:30  
**Transcript:**

**[00:00-01:00] - Intro**
Hey Adrian Soto here with SEO Insider Academy. ChatGPT is changing how we do SEO. Not replacing it, but accelerating it. Today I'm showing you practical ChatGPT prompts that actually work for SEO tasks. We're talking keyword research, content planning, topical authority mapping, and more.

**[01:00-04:30] - ChatGPT for Keyword Research**
First use case: keyword research. Traditional approach: use SEO tools to find keywords. ChatGPT approach: describe your business and audience to ChatGPT, it generates comprehensive keyword ideas. More important: it explains intent and opportunity for each keyword. Example prompt: "I run a B2B project management SaaS targeting finance teams. Generate 50 keywords they search for related to project management with intent classification."

**[04:30-08:00] - Creating Content Clusters and Pillars**
ChatGPT is excellent for planning topical authority. Prompt: "Create a topical cluster for 'project management software' with one pillar page and 8 cluster subtopics for B2B finance teams." ChatGPT generates comprehensive structure with logical relationships. You take this structure and build your content plan around it. Saves hours of brainstorming.

**[08:00-12:15] - Content Outlining and Structure**
Content outlining is where ChatGPT excels. You describe your topic and target audience. ChatGPT creates comprehensive outline with main sections, subsections, and talking points. This outline is 80% of the work. Your writer just fills in the details. Quality improves because outline is comprehensive.

**[12:15-16:30] - Content Generation (With Caution)**
ChatGPT can generate content, but here's the key: it works best when guided. Use ChatGPT to generate initial draft from your detailed outline. Then have a human expert review, fact-check, add specific examples, and refine voice. Pure AI-generated content without human review often contains factual errors and lacks depth.

**[16:30-20:00] - Internal Linking Strategy**
ChatGPT can help with internal linking strategy. Describe your site structure and main topics. Ask: "Create an internal linking strategy for these topics to build topical authority." ChatGPT provides recommendations on which pages should link to which, how many links each page should receive. This is faster than manual planning.

**[20:00-24:00] - Competitive Analysis**
ChatGPT can analyze competitor content. Paste competitor article or describe what they cover. Ask: "What are the gaps in this content? What would I add?" ChatGPT identifies gaps and opportunities. Combined with your actual expertise, this drives better competitive content.

**[24:00-25:30] - Outro**
The key theme: ChatGPT accelerates work when you know how to prompt effectively. It's not magic; it's a tool. Use it strategically, review the output, combine with human expertise. That's how to get best results.

## Video 2: B2B SaaS Content Production Workflow

**Video URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wW3456789JKL  
**Published:** June 8, 2026

**Transcript:**

**[00:00-00:45] - Intro**
I want to walk you through the exact content production workflow we use for B2B SaaS clients. This isn't theory; this is how we actually work. Following this process, small teams can produce high-quality content at scale.

**[00:45-03:30] - Phase 1: Planning and Research**
Step one: customer research. Interviews, surveys, support tickets. What problems do they have? What language do they use? Step two: topic planning. Based on research, identify 10-15 high-priority topics. Step three: keyword research. Find keywords related to these topics. Step four: content calendar. Plan publication schedule and dependencies.

**[03:30-07:00] - Phase 2: Detailed Outlines**
Step five: create detailed outline for each piece. Use ChatGPT to accelerate outline creation. Include: main sections, subsections, data points, examples. Outline is the blueprint; good outline means faster writing.

**[07:00-11:30] - Phase 3: Content Creation**
Step six: AI-assisted drafting. Use ChatGPT or Jasper to create initial draft from outline. Takes 1-2 hours instead of 5-8. Step seven: expert review. Subject matter expert reads draft, fact-checks, adds depth, examples, adjusts voice. This is critical.

**[11:30-15:00] - Phase 4: Optimization and Publication**
Step eight: SEO optimization. Use tools like Surfer SEO to optimize for target keywords. Check readability, structure, keyword placement. Step nine: final edit. Editor reviews for grammar, flow, consistency. Step ten: publication and scheduling.

**[15:00-19:00] - Phase 5: Promotion**
Step eleven: multi-channel promotion. Social media, email, internal links, community participation. Content that's not promoted doesn't drive results. Plan promotion as part of content workflow. Step twelve: measurement. Track traffic, engagement, conversion. Optimize based on results.

**[19:00-22:10] - Team Structure and ROI**
One person can manage this workflow for 3-4 pieces monthly. With a small team of 2-3, you can produce 15+ monthly. The key is process, not heroic effort. Following this, the content ROI is typically 40-50% improvement in organic leads within 6 months.

## Video 3: SEO for B2B SaaS - Complete Strategy Framework

**Video URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xX4567890KLM  
**Published:** June 1, 2026  
**Duration:** 19:45  
**Transcript:**

**[00:00-00:50] - Intro**
B2B SaaS SEO is different than general SEO. Longer sales cycles, multiple decision-makers, higher CLV. This requires strategic thinking. I'm walking through our complete B2B SaaS SEO framework.

**[00:50-04:15] - Understanding Buyer Journey**
First, map buyer journey. Awareness stage: prospect realizes they have a problem. Consideration stage: they're researching solutions. Decision stage: they're choosing between vendors. Each stage has different keywords, different content needs, different conversion actions. Your strategy must address all stages.

**[04:15-08:30] - Keyword Research Aligned with Intent**
Traditional: chase high-volume keywords. B2B SaaS: focus on high-intent keywords at each stage. Awareness stage keywords have lower intent but high volume. Decision stage keywords have lower volume but high intent and conversion. Allocate content resources to decision-stage keywords first because of high ROI.

**[08:30-12:45] - Content Strategy by Buyer Stage**
Awareness content: educational, problem-definition focused. Blog posts, beginner guides. Examples of keywords: "what is project management," "why is project management important." Consideration content: comparative, best practices, how-to guides. Examples: "project management methodologies comparison," "best practices for team coordination." Decision content: product comparison, ROI calculators, case studies, free trials. These keywords typically have product names or "best" in them.

**[12:45-16:30] - Topical Authority and Internal Linking**
Organize content into topical pillars. Don't cover everything; go deep on 2-3 topics. Build topical authority. Link internally to concentrate authority on high-value pages. This improves ranking for competitive keywords.

**[16:30-19:45] - Measurement and Optimization**
Measure impact on business metrics, not just traffic. Track: organic sessions, trial signups, qualified leads, customer acquisition cost. Optimize content that drives highest quality leads. Use these insights to inform future content strategy.
