## Video 1: B2B SaaS Growth Strategy - Content as a Lever

**Video URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yY5678901LMN  
**Published:** June 14, 2026  
**Duration:** 24:00  
**Transcript:**

**[00:00-01:00] - Intro**
Jared Friedman here with Y Combinator. I'm going to talk about how B2B SaaS companies use content for growth. This isn't about marketing; this is about business strategy. Content is a lever that impacts acquisition, retention, and pricing power.

**[01:00-04:30] - The Content Flywheel**
B2B SaaS companies that scale fastest have a content flywheel. Content drives awareness. Aware prospects come to trial. Trials convert to customers. Customers refer and create word-of-mouth. High-quality content also improves retention because users learn how to use the product better. And quality content supports pricing power because it demonstrates value.

**[04:30-08:45] - Content Strategy by Company Stage**
Stage 1 (Pre-product): content establishes expertise and builds early audience. Stage 2 (0-1M ARR): content drives acquisition. Product is good but no one knows about it. Content is accelerant. Stage 3 (1-10M ARR): content establishes category authority and informs product roadmap. Stage 4 (10M+): content is used for retention and upsell in addition to acquisition.

**[08:45-13:00] - Focus Matters**
The biggest mistake is broad content. "Best practices for B2B SaaS." That's too broad. Instead, focus on specific buyer segments and their specific pain points. "Audit workflows for finance teams." That's specific. Specific content ranks better, converts better, and builds authority better.

**[13:00-17:30] - Content-Product Alignment**
Critical: align content with product. Customer pain points in content should be problems the product solves. Sales team feedback informs content. Product roadmap informs content strategy. When these are aligned, growth accelerates because you're sending consistent signals about what you solve and for whom.

**[17:30-21:15] - Measurement**
Measure content impact on business metrics. Not just traffic, leads, or engagement. Measure: trial conversion from different traffic sources, customer lifetime value by source, churn impact of educational content, pricing power. This tells you which content actually impacts business.

**[21:15-24:00] - Outro**
Content is a strategic lever. Focus, align, measure. Those are the keys. For more on this, check out Y Combinator's startup resources. Thanks for watching.

## Video 2: Scaling B2B SaaS - Why Content-Sales-Product Alignment Matters

**Video URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zZ6789012MNO  
**Published:** June 7, 2026  
**Duration:** 20:30  
**Transcript:**

**[00:00-00:50] - Intro**
YC companies I advise often make one mistake: siloing content, product, and sales. These should be tightly integrated. When they are, growth is 3-5x faster. Let me explain why.

**[00:50-04:00] - The Silo Problem**
Traditional structure: content team creates content without product input. Product team builds features without input from content or sales. Sales team figures out messaging on their own. Result: inconsistent messaging, content that doesn't support sales, product features that aren't communicated, slow growth. This is where most companies are.

**[04:00-08:30] - The Integrated Approach**
Better structure: shared customer insights meetings. Sales brings prospect feedback. Product brings roadmap and technical insights. Content brings messaging and positioning. They align on: key customer pain points, competitive positioning, messaging hierarchy, content needs. They iterate together monthly.

**[08:30-13:00] - Real-World Example**
One Y Combinator company we advised was spending $100K/month on ads but struggling with conversion. Why? Content wasn't addressing sales objections. Sales was using messaging product team didn't test. Product team was building features sales wasn't asking for. We aligned them. Same ad spend, 3x conversion rate within 3 months.

**[13:00-17:15] - How to Implement**
Step one: shared quarterly business review with content, sales, product. Shared goals. Step two: monthly alignment meetings. Customer insights, messaging, content planning. Step three: shared OKRs. Measure impact on acquisition together. Step four: rapid feedback loops. Sales shares what's working; content creates more; product considers feature implications.

**[17:15-20:30] - The Result**
Companies that align these three grow faster, retain better, and scale more efficiently. This is foundational to scaling B2B SaaS.
