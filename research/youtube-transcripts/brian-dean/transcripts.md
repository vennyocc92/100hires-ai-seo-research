**[07:30-10:15] - Content Freshness Still Matters**
Content freshness is still a ranking factor, and this update reinforced it. But it's not as simple as updating everything daily. We found that content updated within 30 days shows a 18% ranking boost. But there's diminishing returns—updating multiple times per day doesn't help. The key is intentional, meaningful updates. Fixing outdated information, adding new data, updating statistics. Purely cosmetic updates don't move the needle.

**[17:30-19:30] - Recovery Strategy**
If you were hit, here's what to do. One, audit your E-E-A-T signals. Do your pages have author bios? Do readers know who wrote the content? Two, update content systematically. Brian Dean here. We're halfway through 2026, and if you're still using SEO strategies from 2024, you're falling behind. I want to walk you through the biggest strategic shifts that are happening right now in SEO. These are not tweaks—these are fundamental changes in how you should approach search engine optimization.

**[00:35-03:00] - From Keywords to Topics and Entities**
First major shift: we're moving away from keyword-focused SEO to topic and entity-focused SEO. You might be thinking, "Brian, haven't we heard this before?" Yeah, we have. But it's actually happening now. Here's the difference. Keyword SEO was about optimizing for specific search terms. Topic SEO is about comprehensively covering a topic from multiple angles. Entity SEO is about how your content connects to people, places, organizations, and concepts that Google understands.

**[03:00-06:15] - What Topical Authority Actually Means**
Topical authority means Google sees you as the go-to resource for a topic. Not for specific keywords, but for the entire topic ecosystem. For example, if you're a B2B SaaS company selling project management software, topical authority means covering project management comprehensively: methodologies, best practices, team dynamics, tools, integrations, workflows, compliance, everything.

**[06:15-09:30] - The Shift Away From Keyword Silos**
This means abandoning the old keyword silo structure. You know, where you have one page for "project management software," another for "project management tools," another for "best project management software." That's still fine, but it's not enough. You need pages that explore the relationships between these concepts. How does project management connect to team productivity? To customer satisfaction? To ROI? Google wants to see this comprehensive understanding.

**[09:30-13:00] - AI's Role in Topical Authority**
This is where AI becomes valuable. AI can help you map out topic ecosystems. Tools like Claude and ChatGPT can help you identify all the subtopics and entity relationships within a broad topic. You can then create a content strategy that covers all these relationships. We've seen sites increase organic traffic 40%+ by shifting to this topical authority approach combined with AI-assisted planning.

**[13:00-16:30] - Link Building as Relationship Building**
Second major shift: link building as relationship building, which we talked about in the previous video. But I want to emphasize this as a strategic shift. It's not just a tactic change; it's a worldview change. Links are no longer your primary goal. Relationships are. Links are a byproduct of relationships and great content.

**[16:30-19:45] - Content Quality Bar Keeps Rising**
The third shift: the quality bar for ranking is just getting higher. The average ranking content is better than it was a year ago. You literally have to produce better content to rank. This means better research, better writing, better design, better user experience. AI can help with volume, but humans need to ensure quality.

**[19:45-21:15] - Outro and Action Items**
So the action items: one, audit your content strategy. Are you keyword-focused or topic-focused? Two, map out your topic ecosystem. Three, build relationships in your industry. Four, invest in content quality. These are the big strategic shifts. Full details on our blog. Thanks for watching. Subscribe.
